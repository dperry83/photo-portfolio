# Photography Website Sitemap

This document defines the agreed version 1 sitemap for the founder-focused photography website.

The site is intentionally small. Its job is to help a busy founder quickly understand who the site is for, why the work matters, what kind of experience to expect, and how to reach out. The structure follows the buyer journey from awareness to trust to inquiry.[cite:19][cite:21]

## Version 1 sitemap

The agreed version 1 page set is:

- Home
- Work
- About
- Services
- FAQ
- Contact[cite:19]

## Navigation

The agreed primary navigation is:

- Work
- Services
- About
- FAQ
- Contact

The primary call to action should point toward inquiry or contact, with as little friction as possible. This supports the goal of making the next step feel clear, guided, and low-drama for a busy founder audience.[cite:21][cite:19]

## Page roles

### Home

The homepage should do the most important work quickly:

- State who this is for
- Name the credibility gap
- Explain the practical value of better photos
- Show selected proof
- Make the next step easy[cite:21][cite:19]

### Work

The Work page should show proof, not volume. The goal is to show strong examples that feel useful in real founder contexts such as LinkedIn, websites, decks, PR, and speaking, rather than creating a giant undifferentiated gallery.[cite:21][cite:19]

### About

The About page should build trust by explaining the founder focus, the low-friction approach, and the coaching angle for non-models. It should help a camera-shy founder feel understood rather than sold to.[cite:21][cite:19]

### Services

The Services page should present the offer clearly and simply, including the logic behind a right-sized founder photo set. This page should make the work feel practical and outcome-driven rather than vague or overly brand-heavy.[cite:21][cite:19]

### FAQ

The FAQ page should remove final friction. It should answer the practical and emotional questions that delay inquiry: time, nerves, prep, what to wear, what is delivered, and what the process feels like.[cite:21][cite:19]

### Contact

The Contact page should make reaching out feel small, easy, and guided. It should not feel like a heavy sales step or a complicated application process.[cite:21][cite:19]

## Optional later additions

These are intentionally not part of version 1, but may become useful later:

- Case studies
- Journal / blog
- Founder photo guides
- Resource pages such as what to wear or what photos a founder actually needs[cite:19]

These are future additions because they are more helpful for SEO, authority, and long-term content strategy than they are for getting version 1 live.[cite:19]

## Sitemap principle

Version 1 should stay small enough that each page has a clear job. The site should feel simple, credible, and easy to move through, not bloated or over-explained.[cite:19][cite:21]
