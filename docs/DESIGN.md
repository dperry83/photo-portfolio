# Photography Website Design Direction

This document captures the agreed design direction for version 1 of the founder-focused photography website.

The goal is a site that feels as credible as the businesses the client is building. The design should feel modern, calm, and human, with enough taste to feel considered but not so much styling that it becomes performative or distracting.[cite:21][cite:19]

## Core tone

The agreed visual tone is:

- Credible
- Calm
- Modern
- Human
- Clear
- Low-drama[cite:21][cite:19]

This is not meant to feel corporate, flashy, trendy, or overly precious. The site should feel like a clear business tool with taste.[cite:21]

## Design principles

### Clarity first

The site should explain who it is for, what problem it solves, and what to do next without making the visitor work for it. Layout, copy, and imagery should support fast understanding for busy founders.[cite:21][cite:19]

### Credibility over performance

The design should feel trustworthy and grounded, not artsy for its own sake. The site is not trying to impress other photographers; it is trying to help founders feel that this person understands their world and can help them show up well online.[cite:21]

### Human, not stiff

The design should avoid fake-corporate polish. It should feel relaxed, guided, and modern without becoming cold or generic.[cite:21]

### Restrained visual system

The visual system should rely on strong typography, good spacing, restrained color, and purposeful photography. One accent color is enough. The overall feel should come from consistency and taste rather than decorative effects.[cite:19]

## Recommended visual direction

### Overall mood

Calm confidence.

The site should feel composed and capable, with room to breathe, but not so sparse that it feels empty or aloof.[cite:21]

### Typography

Typography should be modern, readable, and slightly elevated. The type system should support credibility first, with just enough character to avoid feeling generic. Strong hierarchy matters more than showing off.[cite:19]

### Color

Use restrained neutrals plus one accent color. The palette should support clarity and trust, not trendiness or visual noise.[cite:19]

### Layout

The layout should be clean and spacious, but not overly minimal. It should feel structured and intentional, with strong rhythm and simple navigation.[cite:19]

### Imagery

Photography should be used purposefully. Images should support the story of real founder-facing use cases rather than acting as filler or pure decoration.[cite:21]

## What to avoid

Avoid these directions in version 1:

- Trendy startup gradients
- Loud visual effects
- Generic SaaS aesthetics
- Moody luxury photography clichés
- Over-designed art direction that distracts from the offer
- Sparse minimalism that feels cold[cite:19][cite:21]

## Messaging and design alignment

The design should reinforce the same message as the copy:

- This is for founders
- This solves a credibility problem, not a vanity problem
- The process is guided and low-stress
- The final images have practical jobs to do[cite:21][cite:19]

## Version 1 design standard

If a design choice makes the site feel clearer, calmer, and more credible, it is probably on track. If it makes the site feel trendier, louder, or more abstract, it is probably off track.[cite:19][cite:21]
